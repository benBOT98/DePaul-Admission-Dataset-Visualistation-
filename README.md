# DePaul Adaminssion Dataset Visualisation 

## Objectives

The main objective of this dataset is to:
 - explore the dataset
 - assess the quality of the dataset
 - identify trends and patterns of the data set, and
 - create dashboard visualisations to support findings

## Tools

The tools used are:
  1. Excel (Power Query) - for data cleaning
  2. Power BI - for data visualisation

## Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

The dashboard indicates that DePaul University's admissions are heavily dependent on applicants from India, while application volumes have been declining since their peak in early 2024. Additionally, incomplete documentation remains a major challenge in the admission process. Expanding recruitment efforts, streamlining document submission, and addressing the declining application trend would likely improve both application volume and admission outcomes.

## Insights 

Key Insights from the DePaul Admission Dashboard
 
 1. India Dominates Application Volume

India contributed approximately 2.7K applications, accounting for more than half of the total 5K applicants. This is significantly higher than the United States (0.5K), Ghana (0.4K), and Nigeria (0.4K), indicating a strong concentration of applicants from a single country.

 2. Admission Funnel Shows Moderate Conversion

Out of 5,000 total applicants, only 3,000 were admitted, resulting in an admission rate of 65%. This means that roughly 35% of applicants were not admitted, suggesting room to improve applicant quality or admission conversion processes.

 3. Missing Documents Are a Major Bottleneck

The Contact Outcome Distribution reveals that 31.67% of applicants have missing or incomplete transcripts, making it the second-largest category after follow-up cases (36.99%). This indicates that document submission issues are a significant barrier to admission processing.

 4. Applications Peaked in Early 2024 and Declined Afterwards

Application volume reached its highest point in January 2024 (521 applications) before experiencing a steady decline through 2025, dropping to only 65 applications in the latest period. This suggests a reduction in recruitment effectiveness, market demand, or outreach activities over time.

## Recommendations

 1. Diversify Recruitment Efforts Across More Countries

Since India contributes the majority of applications, DePaul should increase recruitment campaigns in countries such as Nigeria, Ghana, Pakistan, and the United States to reduce dependence on a single market and broaden its applicant base.

 2. Improve Applicant Support for Document Submission

Implement automated reminders, document checklists, and applicant support services to reduce the high percentage of missing transcripts and incomplete applications. This could significantly improve admission conversion rates.

 3. Investigate the Declining Application Trend

Conduct a detailed analysis of the sharp decline in applications after January 2024. Review marketing effectiveness, admission requirements, competitor activities, and economic factors to identify and address the root causes of the downturn.
